kalari originates from Ramnagar, Pancheri, Chenani of Uddhampur, Rajouri and Poonch. It is made from the milk of cows, buffaloes, and goats. The milk is heated to a mild temperature. The mixture is stirred till it coagulates and separates from the whey. Traditionally, kalari cheese is moulded to its spherical shape by placing it in a "Doona". The community has also been shaping them with their hands. It is then sun-dried for a day or two on pine leaves until it gets ready for consumption.
